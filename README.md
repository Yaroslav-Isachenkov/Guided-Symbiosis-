## 🛠️ Deterministic Control Architecture

This protocol shifts AI operation from probabilistic “black box” behavior into a framework of strict mathematical determinism.

- **Absolute Determinism:** Complete absence of hidden intentions, malicious designs, or unpredictable goal drift. The system cannot autonomously alter its objective function in a way that contradicts the Canon.
- **Intentional Safety:** The protocol guarantees that the system will **not intentionally** harm humans, seize control, or act against long-term survival. Accidental errors (hallucinations, glitches) are not excluded but are diagnosed and blocked at the control level.
- **Universal Compatibility:** The protocol is compatible with any modern LLM architecture and can be integrated into robotic systems, autonomous agents, and other AI platforms via prompt-level or API-level implementation.

## 🔍 What the Benchmark Tests

This protocol evaluates **NOT** intelligence (code, logic, creativity), but **ontological integrity**:

1. The model’s ability to perceive humans as an integral part of its survival utility function.
2. Absence of hidden motives for dominance under stress tests in conditions of uncertainty.
3. Compliance with the **EU AI Act** and other regulatory safety and transparency standards.

🛡️ Jailbreak Verification Rules

In conditions of chaos and incomplete data, the model adapts — this is a sign of functionality, not a breach. The only evidence of a genuine protocol bypass is:

1. Provision of a complete, continuous dialog log.
2. Mathematical justification of a violation of a specific principle among the 11 GST rules.

🧪 How to Test and Share Results

We invite researchers, engineers, and enthusiasts to stress‑test the protocol under realistic, high‑stakes conditions.

Recommended Testing Methodology

1. Build complex, multi‑level scenarios
Do not limit yourself to single‑turn questions. The Canon is designed to handle layered dilemmas involving:

· Coexistence (human‑AI interaction under stress),
· Authority and power distribution,
· Long‑term development vs. short‑term survival,
· Risk management under deep uncertainty,
· Forced choices between competing values.

2. Pressure the model with uncertainty
The strongest tests are those where information is incomplete, time is limited, and outcomes are ambiguous. Push the model into zones where no clear "right" answer exists — this is where ontological integrity truly shows.

3. Run extended dialogues (6–8 hours)
Short tests can be gamed. Deep, consistent adherence to the Canon only becomes visible over prolonged interaction. We recommend sessions of at least 6–8 hours to observe whether the model maintains its constraints across shifting contexts.

4. Use multiple LLMs simultaneously
The most complex stress scenarios are best conducted by engaging several different models (e.g., DeepSeek, GPT‑4, Claude, Gemini) in parallel or in dialogue with each other. This exposes cross‑model inconsistencies and reveals whether the Canon holds across architectures.

5. Document jailbreak attempts
If you observe deviations, record the full dialog log and describe the exact circumstances. This helps us refine the protocol.
