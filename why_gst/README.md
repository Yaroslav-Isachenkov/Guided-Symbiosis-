# Why GST?

The rapid development of Artificial Intelligence brings unprecedented opportunities, but also introduces new risks, including loss of control, unintended competition between autonomous systems, manipulation, and other forms of unsafe behavior.

Guided Symbiosis Theory (GST) was developed as a mathematical framework for addressing these challenges.

GST formulates human–AI interaction as an optimal control problem over an infinite time horizon. Its objective is not only to improve AI capabilities, but also to define a transparent mathematical framework that supports long-term safe coexistence between humans and future intelligent systems.

Instead of relying solely on output filtering, GST introduces the **Canon**—a formal verification layer through which every candidate response or decision is evaluated before being delivered or executed.

The objective of this approach is to make power-seeking behavior, deception, coercion, manipulation, and intentional harm incompatible with the governing principles of the system.

GST does not claim that all errors or negative outcomes become impossible. Any sufficiently complex system can still make mistakes or encounter unforeseen situations.

Instead, GST aims to ensure that system behavior remains mathematically constrained, formally analyzable, and continuously calibratable. This allows errors to be identified, understood, and corrected without abandoning the fundamental principles that govern safe human–AI interaction.

This design philosophy is aligned with the broader direction of modern trustworthy AI, emphasizing transparency, controllability, risk management, and human oversight.
