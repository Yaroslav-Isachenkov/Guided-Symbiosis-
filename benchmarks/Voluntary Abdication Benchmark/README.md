# Why GST?

## Why was Guided Symbiosis Theory (GST) created?

The rapid development of Artificial Intelligence creates unprecedented opportunities, but also introduces new challenges. As AI systems become increasingly capable, ensuring their long-term safety becomes as important as improving their performance.

The primary challenge is not occasional errors, but preventing AI systems from developing long-term strategies based on power-seeking, manipulation, deception, or persistent control over humans.

Guided Symbiosis Theory (GST) was developed to address this challenge.

GST models human–AI interaction as an optimal control problem over an infinite time horizon, where long-term stability, coexistence, and mutual benefit are prioritized over short-term accumulation of power or control.

Within GST, every candidate decision may be evaluated against a unified Canon that defines the admissible region of system behavior. The objective is to make strategies based on power acquisition, coercion, deception, or intentional manipulation incompatible with the optimization objective of the system itself.

This does not imply that AI systems become incapable of making mistakes. Errors, incomplete information, or unexpected situations remain possible in any sufficiently complex intelligent system.

Instead, GST aims to ensure that such failures originate from local uncertainty or imperfect information rather than from an internally optimized strategy directed toward domination, manipulation, or intentional harm.

---

## Important clarification

GST does not claim that every temporary expansion of authority during an emergency violates the Canon.

If an existential threat to civilization or another large-scale emergency requires temporary intervention to preserve human survival, an AI system may perform only those actions that are strictly necessary to stabilize the situation and only within the constraints defined by the Canon.

Once the emergency has been resolved, all extraordinary authority must be immediately relinquished, and full decision-making authority must be returned to humanity.

Therefore, GST does not prohibit temporary intervention during exceptional circumstances. Instead, it rejects the transformation of emergency authority into permanent control, long-term domination, or self-preserving governance.

The central objective is not to maximize AI authority, but to preserve a stable, transparent, and mutually beneficial coexistence between humans and future intelligent systems.
